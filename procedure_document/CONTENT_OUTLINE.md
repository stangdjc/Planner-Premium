### II.A: What is Microsoft Planner Premium?

**Content Overview:**
Planner Premium is a user-friendly project management tool built on Microsoft Planner that helps teams track and coordinate New Product Introduction (NPI) projects across global and regional teams. It simplifies task organization with features like summary tasks, sub-tasks, and clear timelines, allowing easy visualization of project progress and dependencies. Integrated with Microsoft Teams, it supports seamless communication within project cards. Unlike complex traditional tools, Planner Premium focuses on collaboration, quick updates, and tailored fields to fit NPI workflows, making it easier to manage multiple regions and operating units efficiently.

**Subsections:**

#### II.A.1: Why We Use Planner Premium for NPI Projects
- Historical context: Evolution from previous tools
- Specific benefits for NPI workflow:
  - Multi-region visibility and coordination
  - Integration with Planisware for financial tracking
  - Teams-native communication
  - Custom field flexibility for NPI governance
  - Timeline and dependency visualization
  - Audit trail and change history
- Comparison: Planner Premium vs. traditional project management tools (simplified comparison table)
- ROI and investment justification
- Alignment with Medtronic's digital transformation goals

#### II.A.2: Key Differences from Microsoft Planner Basic
- Feature comparison table:
  - Timeline view (Premium only)
  - Shared plans and dependencies (Premium)
  - Custom fields (limited in Basic, expanded in Premium)
  - Project Manager Agent (Premium 2025)
  - Premium templates (Premium)
  - Advanced reporting (Premium)

#### II.A.3: What Planner Premium is NOT

### II.B: Core Concepts & Terminology

**Purpose:** Establish shared language across all audiences

**Subsections:**

#### II.B.1: NPI Lifecycle Phases (End-to-End Overview)
- **Pipeline/Initiate:** Initial concept, submitted for global review
  - Duration: Variable
  - Owner: Initiating OU
  - Deliverable: Upstream card in Pipeline bucket
  
- **Global Alignment (GA):** Global stakeholder alignment on service model
  - Duration: 8-12 weeks
  - Owner: Main PM
  - Deliverable: Completed Playbook Section 1
  
- **Regional Alignment (RA):** Regional forecast and service model confirmation
  - Duration: 4-8 weeks
  - Owner: Regional PM
  - Deliverable: Regional cards created, Playbook Section 2 complete
  
- **Regional Engage (RE):** Pre-engagement with internal teams
  - Duration: 4-8 weeks
  - Owner: Regional PM
  - Deliverable: SME names, initial commitment received
  
- **Regional Kick-off (RKO):** Project initiation with regional teams
  - Duration: 2-4 weeks
  - Owner: Regional PM + SMEs
  - Deliverable: Timelines, roles assigned, project plan reviewed
  
- **Execute:** Implementation and deliverable creation
  - Duration: Varies (typically 4-12 months)
  - Owner: Regional PM + SME team
  - Deliverable: Training, documentation, process changes completed
  
- **Hypercare/Launch:** Go-live support and stabilization
  - Duration: 30 days post-launch
  - Owner: Regional PM + Support team
  - Deliverable: Issues resolved, launch success confirmed
  
- **Close-out:** Project formal closure and lessons learned
  - Duration: 1-2 weeks
  - Owner: Regional PM + Main PM
  - Deliverable: Lessons learned, resources released

#### II.B.2: Planner Premium Terminology
- **Board:** Container for all project cards (ex: "PLC - RR - Calima - 01 Singapore")
- **Card:** Individual task or work item (Upstream, Summary, Sub-task)
- **Upstream Card:** Project-level summary card (represents entire project)
- **Downstream Card:** Regional or operational task card (supports Upstream)
- **Bucket:** Container within a board (Pipeline, GA, RA, Engage, Kick-off, Execute, Hypercare, Close-out)
- **Summary Task:** Multi-level card that contains sub-tasks and shows overall progress
- **Sub-task:** Granular work item under a Summary task
- **Timeline View:** Gantt-like visualization showing schedules and dependencies
- **Dependencies:** Relationships between cards (Finish-to-Start, Start-to-Start, etc.)
- **Custom Field:** NPI-specific data field (ex: "Regional Complexity," "Playbook Status")
- **Goal:** Strategic grouping (NPI, RPE, EOL, Enhancement) for filtering and reporting
- **Sprint:** Time-based grouping for focused work periods
- **Loop:** Dynamic content card that summarizes and links to other cards in Teams
- **Project Manager Agent:** AI-powered tool for progress updates and insights (Premium feature)

#### II.B.3: Organizational Structure & Roles
- **Main Product Line (PLC):** Global program owner
  - Role: Main PM (drives GA, coordinates across regions)

- **Operating Unit (OU) / iOU:** Regional business unit
  - Role: Regional Service Leader, People Leader

- **Regional PM:** Coordinates regional readiness activities
  - Role: Regional PM, Regional Service Coordinator

- **Subject Matter Expert (SME):** Functional expert (training, service, ops, etc.)
  - Role: Training Specialist, Service Manager, Ops Manager
  - Responsibility: Deliverable creation, timeline input, execution
  
- **Portfolio Lead / PMO:** Oversees multiple projects
  - Role: Portfolio Lead, Project Management Office contact
  - Responsibility: Cross-project visibility, governance, escalation
  
- **Playbook Owner:** Content manager for Global NPI Playbook
  - Role: Dedicated Playbook Manager or Main PM

### III.B: Understanding the NPI Card Structure

#### III.B.1: Upstream Card (Project-Level Summary)
**What it is:** One card per NPI project (global level)
**Who creates it:** Main PM (in Pipeline bucket)
**Who updates it:** Main PM primarily, Regional PMs add regional-specific updates

**Card Structure Overview:**
- Title: "NPI - [PLC Name] - [Project Code]" (ex: "NPI - Calima - CAL-001")
- Description: Executive summary of the project
- Assigned to: Main PM
- Start date: Concept date or go-live date (depends on organization preference)
- Due date: Launch date
- Bucket: Moves from Pipeline ‚Üí GA ‚Üí RA ‚Üí Engage ‚Üí Kick-off ‚Üí Execute ‚Üí Hypercare ‚Üí Close-out
- Priority: High/Important (most NPI projects)
- Progress: Visually shows completion % as regional cards are completed
- Sub-tasks: Optional summary level activities

**Reading an Upstream Card:**
- Click the card to open detail view
- Scan the custom fields for key info:
  - Planisware ID (links to financial data)
  - Global Service Model (what we're delivering)
  - Launch Date (when it happens)
  - Regions Involved (how many to coordinate)
  - Complexity Rating (how complex is this?)
- Review attachments for project summary or business case
- Check progress bar (% of regions at each phase)

**When you'll interact with Upstream cards:**
- Reading for context about the overall project
- Checking global timelines
- Understanding dependencies with other projects
- Reporting status up to leadership

#### III.B.2: Regional Card (Operating Unit-Specific)
**What it is:** One card per region per project
**Who creates it:** Regional PM (during Regional Alignment phase)
**Who updates it:** Regional PM (owns it), SMEs (contribute to timelines and status)

**Card Structure:**
- Title: "[PLC Name] - [Region] - Regional Readiness" (ex: "Calima - Singapore - Regional Readiness")
- Description: Regional service model, scope, key considerations
- Assigned to: Regional PM
- Start date: RA phase start date
- Due date: Regional Go-Live date (may differ from global launch)
- Bucket: Moves from RA ‚Üí Engage ‚Üí Kick-off ‚Üí Execute ‚Üí Hypercare ‚Üí Close-out
- Progress: Increases as SME deliverables are completed
- Sub-tasks: Regional deliverables (Training, SOPs, etc.)
- Dependencies: Linked to Upstream card and may depend on other regional cards

**Reading a Regional Card:**
- Understand the regional scope and timeline
- See assigned sub-tasks and upcoming deliverables
- Check regional complexity rating
- Review risks and issues specific to region
- Understand dependencies (what must happen in other regions first?)

**When you'll interact with Regional cards:**
- Daily work on regional deliverables
- Tracking SME progress
- Identifying blockers or risks
- Updating status for governance calls

#### III.B.3: Sub-Tasks (Functional Deliverables)
**What they are:** Individual work items under a card
**Who creates them:** Regional PM or Main PM (should be templated)
**Who updates them:** Assigned SME (Training, Service, Ops, etc.)

**Sub-Task Structure:**
- Title: Specific deliverable (ex: "Create Training Materials - Service Techs", "Develop Standard Operating Procedure - Regional Service")
- Assigned to: Functional SME responsible
- Due date: When that deliverable is needed
- Checklist items: Optional breakdown of sub-task
- Notes: Context, links to templates, requirements

**Common Sub-Task Templates:**
- **Training Deliverables:** Training plan, training materials, trainer certification, facilitation of training
- **Service Deliverables:** Service procedures, spare parts readiness, field service training, first repair protocols
- **Operational Deliverables:** Standard operating procedures, inventory management, scheduling procedures
- **Marketing/Communications:** Launch communications, internal announcements, customer updates
- **Business Process Changes:** Workflow changes, system configuration, approval routing

**Reading a Sub-Task:**
- See what the deliverable is
- Who owns it
- When it's due
- Current status (not started, in progress, completed)
- Any blockers or notes

**When you'll work with sub-tasks:**
- Assigned to you? Then you execute and update the sub-task
- Part of your team? Then you track if team members are completing theirs
- Project manager? Then you ensure all sub-tasks are completed on time

### III.C: Day-to-Day Tasks & Card Management

#### III.C.1: Creating a New Card (Regional PM perspective)

**Scenario:** It's time to create your regional card (during Regional Alignment phase)

**Step-by-step:**
1. **Navigate to the Upstream card** for your project
2. **Look at the regional card template** (your Main PM should provide this or it should be in Teams folder)
3. **Click "+ Add card"** in the RA bucket (or relevant bucket)
4. **Fill in required fields:**
   - **Title:** "[PLC] - [Region] - Regional Readiness"
   - **Assigned to:** You (Regional PM)
   - **Start date:** When your RA starts
   - **Due date:** Regional launch date (or phase end date)
   - **Description:** Copy from template, customize for your region
5. **Save the card**
6. **Create the link to Upstream card:** (See section III.C.5 on dependencies)
7. **Add custom fields:** (See section III.D on custom fields)
8. **Create sub-tasks:** (See section III.C.2)
9. **Share with your team** by adding them to the card or Teams channel

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- ‚ùå Creating regional card before RA phase officially starts
- ‚ùå Forgetting to link to Upstream card
- ‚ùå Using non-standard naming convention
- ‚ùå Not filling in required custom fields
- ‚ùå Creating sub-tasks without clear ownership

#### III.C.2: Creating Sub-Tasks (Functional deliverables)

**Scenario:** You need to break down your regional card into specific deliverables

**Step-by-step:**
1. **Open your regional card**
2. **Look for "Sub-tasks" section** (or "Checklist")
3. **Click "+ Add sub-task"**
4. **Fill in:**
   - **Title:** Specific deliverable name
   - **Assigned to:** The functional SME (Training lead, Service manager, etc.)
   - **Due date:** When that deliverable is needed (usually before Kick-off)
   - **Notes:** Context, links to templates, requirements
5. **Click "Add"** to create
6. **Notify the assigned SME** (in Teams or email) that they have a new sub-task
7. **Set a reminder** to follow up if sub-task isn't started 2 weeks before due date

**Template Sub-Tasks (Copy & Customize):**
- [ ] Create Training Materials - [Function Area] - Due: [X weeks before KO]
- [ ] Develop Standard Operating Procedure - [Function Area] - Due: [X weeks before KO]
- [ ] Identify Required Spare Parts & Inventory - Due: [6 weeks before launch]
- [ ] Schedule Launch Day Communications - Due: [2 weeks before launch]
- [ ] Confirm Regional Readiness (sign-off) - Due: [1 week before launch]

#### III.C.3: Updating Card Status & Progress

**Daily/Weekly Routine:**

**For cards assigned to you (Regional PM):**
1. **Check "My Tasks"** view every morning
2. **For each card/sub-task assigned to you:**
   - Is it still on track? Yes ‚Üí Move to next priority
   - Is it blocked? Yes ‚Üí Escalate (see escalation section)
   - Is it complete? Yes ‚Üí Mark complete (see below)
3. **Update the "Next Activity Date"** field with your next action
4. **Add a comment in Teams** if significant update (governance call reminder, blocker alert, etc.)

**Marking a Sub-Task Complete:**
1. **Open the sub-task**
2. **Click the checkmark or "Mark complete"** button
3. **Add completion notes** if relevant
4. **Notify the team** (sub-task completion may unblock others)

**Updating Overall Card Progress:**
- In Grid view, you'll see a progress bar
- Progress is auto-calculated from sub-task completion % 
- **If progress isn't updating correctly:** Check that sub-tasks are actually marked complete (not just "in progress")

**Weekly Status Update (for Regional PM):**
1. **Review all your regional cards** (use "My Tasks" filter)
2. **Update "Next Activity Date"** on each card
3. **Check all sub-tasks** - any at risk? Any blocked?
4. **Add a comment** summarizing the week's progress
5. **Send a quick update** to your Main PM and OU contact
   - Format: "On track" | "Minor Risk" | "Major Risk" + 1-2 sentence explanation

#### III.C.4: Moving Cards Between Buckets (Phase Progression)

**What it means:** When a project moves from one phase to the next (ex: GA ‚Üí RA), the card physically moves to a new Bucket

**Who can move cards:** Main PM (Upstream), Regional PM (Regional cards)

**Step-by-step (in Board view):**
1. **Find the card** you want to move
2. **Drag the card** to the next Bucket (or right-click ‚Üí Move)
3. **Verify the move** - the card now appears in new Bucket
4. **Update relevant fields:**
   - Start date (first day of new phase)
   - Due date (last day of new phase, usually = launch or gate date)
5. **Add a comment** explaining why it moved (ex: "Moved to Engage - GA complete, regional SME names received")
6. **Notify the team** in Teams that phase has progressed

**Before You Move a Card - Checklist:**
- ‚úì Are all required fields populated?
- ‚úì Have all sub-tasks for this phase been started/completed?
- ‚úì Is the next phase's timeline realistic?
- ‚úì Have key stakeholders approved (Main PM, OU contact)?
- ‚úì Are all risks documented?

**Common Phase Progression:**
1. **Pipeline ‚Üí GA:** Initiated by Main PM when project is approved
2. **GA ‚Üí RA:** Initiated by Main PM when global service model is agreed
3. **RA ‚Üí Engage:** Initiated by Regional PM when regional service model is confirmed
4. **Engage ‚Üí Kick-off:** Initiated by Regional PM when SME commitments are received
5. **Kick-off ‚Üí Execute:** Initiated by Regional PM when project plan is approved
6. **Execute ‚Üí Hypercare:** Initiated by Regional PM when go-live is reached
7. **Hypercare ‚Üí Close-out:** Initiated by Regional PM when 30-day monitoring is complete

**If you move a card backward:** ‚ö†Ô∏è This is a flag! It usually means something wasn't ready. Make a note and escalate to your Main PM.

#### III.C.5: Understanding & Creating Dependencies

**What is a dependency?** One card depends on another card finishing first (or starting at the same time)

**Why it matters:** Helps you see what work blocks what, and avoid surprises

**Dependency Types in Planner:**
- **Finish-to-Start (FS):** Card A must finish before Card B starts (most common)
  - Example: Global Alignment ‚Üí Regional Alignment
- **Start-to-Start (SS):** Card A must start before Card B can start
  - Example: Multiple regions starting training on same week
- **Finish-to-Finish (FF):** Card A must finish before Card B can finish
  - Example: All regions must complete their deliverables before project closing
- **Start-to-Finish (SF):** Card A must start before Card B can finish (rare)
  - Example: Hypercare must start before Execute can officially close

**How to Create a Dependency:**

1. **Open the card** that depends on another
2. **Find the "Dependencies" section** (usually near the bottom)
3. **Click "+ Add dependency"**
4. **Select the relationship type** (Finish-to-Start, etc.)
5. **Search for the card** it depends on (by name or project)
6. **Save the dependency**
7. **Verify** in Timeline view that the dates now show the relationship

**Reading Dependencies in Timeline View:**
- **Vertical lines connecting cards** = dependencies
- **Red lines** = dependency broken (predecessor is late, dependent card is now at risk)
- **Green lines** = dependency is healthy

**Best Practice:** Link each Regional card to its Upstream card (Finish-to-Start: Upstream's GA phase must finish before Regional card starts)

### III.D: Custom Fields & Required Information

**Purpose:** Understand the NPI-specific data fields that track project governance

#### III.D.1: Overview of Custom Fields
- What are custom fields?
  - Extra data columns specific to NPI projects (not in regular Planner)
  - Help track governance, complexity, stakeholder info
  - Limited to 10 per board (all are being used)
- Why we use them
  - Standardize how we track NPI projects across all regions
  - Enable reporting and portfolio visibility
  - Show progress toward regional readiness
- How to populate them
  - Some are filled at card creation (by PM)
  - Some are filled as you progress (by Regional PM or SME)
  - Some are reference-only (you read but don't change)

#### III.D.2: Custom Field Definitions & Usage

**Field #1: Planisware ID** (Required for Upstream & Regional cards)
- **What it is:** Unique identifier linking to Planisware financial tracking
- **Who fills it:** Main PM (Upstream), Regional PM (Regional cards)
- **When it's due:** Upstream = in GA, Regional = in RA
- **Format:** [3-letter OU]-[4-digit project code]-[2-letter region] (ex: SVC-0145-SG)
- **If you don't know it:** Ask your Main PM or OU contact
- **Why it matters:** Finance, Portfolio reporting, Capacity planning

**Field #2: Global Service Model** (Upstream card only, filled in GA)
- **What it is:** Description of what the product service model is globally
- **Options:** Customer Pays | Included | Limited | Engineering Support | Customized Training
- **Who fills it:** Main PM
- **When it's due:** End of GA
- **Why it matters:** Determines what regions can offer (not all models available everywhere)

**Field #3: Regional Service Model** (Regional cards only)
- **What it is:** What service model this region will deliver
- **Options:** Customer Pays | Included | Limited | Engineering Support | Customized Training
- **Who fills it:** Regional PM (with Regional Service Leader approval)
- **When it's due:** End of RA phase
- **Why it matters:** Determines regional deliverables, training scope, resource needs

**Field #4: Complexity Rating** (Upstream & Regional cards)
- **What it is:** How complex is this project for the region?
- **Scale:** Small (SM) | Medium (Med) | Large (LRG) | Extra Large (XL)
- **Who fills it:** Main PM (Upstream), Regional PM (Regional)
- **When it's due:** Upstream = in GA, Regional = in RA
- **Sizing criteria:**
  - **SM:** Single service model, < 5 locations, minimal training
  - **Med:** 1-2 service models, 5-10 locations, standard training
  - **LRG:** Mixed models, 10+ locations, significant training, some workflow changes
  - **XL:** Multiple models, 20+ locations, major training, complex workflow changes
- **Why it matters:** Determines resource allocation, timeline feasibility, risk level

**Field #5: Regions Involved** (Upstream card)
- **What it is:** Count of how many regions must complete this project
- **Format:** Comma-separated region list (ex: "Singapore, India, Australia, Vietnam")
- **Who fills it:** Main PM
- **When it's due:** In GA (updated as regional cards are created)
- **Why it matters:** Portfolio visibility, resource planning

**Field #6: Launch Date (Global)** (Upstream card)
- **What it is:** Target launch date globally
- **Format:** MM/DD/YYYY
- **Who fills it:** Main PM
- **When it's due:** In Pipeline (may change through project)
- **Why it matters:** Critical path planning, resource scheduling, market timing

**Field #7: Launch Date (Regional)** (Regional cards)
- **What it is:** Target launch date for this specific region (may differ from global)
- **Format:** MM/DD/YYYY
- **Who fills it:** Regional PM
- **When it's due:** In RA (must be confirmed by OU contact)
- **Why it matters:** Regional resource planning, SME availability

**Field #8: Playbook Status** (Regional cards)
- **What it is:** Which Playbook sections are complete?
- **Options:** Section 1 Pending | Section 1 Complete | Section 2 Pending | Section 2 Complete | Section 3 Pending | Section 3 Complete | Section 4 Pending | Section 4 Complete
- **Who fills it:** Regional PM (tracks progress as team completes sections)
- **When updated:** Ongoing as you progress through RA, Engage, Kick-off
- **Why it matters:** Shows readiness, governance approval gate, regional compliance

**Field #9: Risk Status** (Upstream & Regional cards)
- **What it is:** Current risk level
- **Options:** Green (On Track) | Yellow (Minor Risk) | Red (Major Risk) | Blocked
- **Who fills it:** Regional PM or Main PM (whoever owns the card)
- **When updated:** Weekly (or immediately if status changes)
- **Example risks:**
  - Yellow: One regional SME delayed, can catch up
  - Red: Key SME not available, timeline now unrealistic
  - Blocked: Waiting for another project to complete, can't proceed
- **Why it matters:** Governance visibility, escalation trigger

**Field #10: Stakeholder Contact** (Regional cards)
- **What it is:** Name + email of OU contact (regional decision-maker)
- **Format:** "Name (Title) - email@company.com"
- **Who fills it:** Regional PM (gets from OU contact)
- **When it's due:** In RA
- **Example:** "Maria Santos (Regional Service Leader) - maria.santos@company.com"
- **Why it matters:** Escalation, approvals, decision-making

### III.E: Tracking Progress & Timelines

#### III.E.1: Understanding Your Card Timeline

**What you'll see:**
- **Start date:** First day of current phase
- **Due date:** Last day of phase (usually = launch date or gate deadline)
- **Remaining days:** Countdown to due date (shown in card)
- **Progress bar:** Percentage of sub-tasks completed

**Reading the Timeline:**
- Green card = On track (due date is in future, progress is steady)
- Yellow card = At risk (due date is approaching, progress is slow)
- Red card = Overdue or Major risk (due date has passed or significant blocker)

#### III.E.2: Using Timeline View for Schedule Management

**When to use Timeline view:**
- Planning your project schedule
- Seeing critical path (what must happen when)
- Identifying bottlenecks (what's blocking what?)
- Communicating timeline to stakeholders

**How to read Timeline view:**
1. **Find your card** on the timeline
2. **See the bar** representing from start date to due date
3. **Look for red lines** connecting cards = dependencies
4. **Red lines** mean: Task on left must finish before task on right starts
5. **If a line is red**, it means the predecessor is at risk

**Example scenario:**
- Global Alignment card (ends 3/15)
- Regional Alignment card (starts 3/20, depends on GA)
- If GA runs late past 3/15, the dependency line turns red = RA is now at risk

#### III.E.3: Next Activity Date (Critical Field)

**What it is:** Your next planned action on this card

**Why it matters:**
- Helps PM see what's coming next
- Signals if work is stalled
- Helps you prioritize your week
- Used in governance reporting

**How to use it:**
1. **Update every Friday** with next week's activity
2. **Format:** MM/DD/YYYY (or "TBD" if uncertain)
3. **Examples:**
   - "01/20/2025 - Schedule SME kickoff call"
   - "01/23/2025 - Review training draft from team"
   - "TBD - Waiting for marketing review"
4. **If you don't update it:** PM will ask "What's next?" in governance call

### III.G: Common Scenarios & How to Handle Them

#### III.G.1: Scenario 1 - You're a Regional PM Creating Your First Card

**Situation:** You've just been assigned as Regional PM. You need to create your regional card.

**What to do:**
1. **Get the template:** Ask your Main PM for the regional card template (should be in Teams or shared folder)
2. **Copy the template to your board:** Right-click the template card ‚Üí Copy card ‚Üí Paste in RA bucket
3. **Fill in custom fields:**
   - Planisware ID: Ask Main PM
   - Regional Service Model: Confirm with your Regional Service Leader
   - Complexity Rating: Rate your region honestly
   - Launch Date: Confirm with OU contact
   - Stakeholder Contact: You (or your OU contact)
4. **Create sub-tasks:** For each deliverable your team needs to complete (training, SOPs, etc.)
5. **Assign sub-tasks:** To the functional SME who will do the work
6. **Share with your team:** Add them to the card and Teams channel
7. **Send a kick-off note:** "Regional card is live. Here's what we need to complete by [date]. Please check your assigned sub-tasks."

#### III.G.2: Scenario 2 - A Sub-Task Owner Isn't Responding

**Situation:** You assigned a Training SME to create training materials (sub-task due in 3 weeks). It's been 2 weeks with no progress.

**What to do:**
1. **Check the sub-task:** Is it marked "In Progress"? If not, follow up.
2. **Send a friendly reminder:** "Hi [Name], I assigned you 'Create Training Materials' which is due [date]. Can you give me a quick update on progress?"
3. **If no response in 2 days:** Escalate to their manager or the Regional PM
4. **If truly blocked:** Document it as a Risk (sub-task status = Red/Blocked)
5. **Update the governance call:** Mention the blocker and proposed solution
6. **Adjust timeline if needed:** If the SME can't do it, either:
   - Assign to someone else
   - Reduce scope
   - Extend timeline (with approval)

#### III.G.3: Scenario 3 - Go-Live Date Just Got Moved Up

**Situation:** Your regional launch was scheduled for 6/15 but marketing needs it done by 5/15 (1 month earlier).

**What to do:**
1. **Reality check:** Can your team really deliver in 1 month? Or is this risky?
2. **Talk to your Regional Service Leader:** What has to give? (Scope, resources, quality?)
3. **Update the card:**
   - Change "Launch Date (Regional)" to 5/15
   - Update all sub-task due dates to move forward
   - Add comment explaining the change
4. **Add a Risk:** Escalate to Main PM and governance team if timeline is now unrealistic
5. **Re-communicate with your team:** Make sure they know the new timeline
6. **Track closely:** With compressed timelines, risks increase

#### III.G.4: Scenario 4 - You Discovered We're Missing a Requirement

**Situation:** You're in Kick-off and realize the regional service model requires a new certification that no one has completed.

**What to do:**
1. **Immediately escalate:** Tell your Regional PM/Main PM
2. **Create a Risk:** Add to the card with high priority
3. **Don't ignore it:** This will come back as a problem on launch day
4. **Options to solve:**
   - Accelerate certification (bring trainer in early)
   - Reduce scope (start without that certification, phase it in)
   - Delay launch (move 5/15 back to 6/15)
5. **Escalate to leadership:** Regional Service Leader needs to decide what to do
6. **Document the decision:** Add to card comments so everyone knows what was decided
